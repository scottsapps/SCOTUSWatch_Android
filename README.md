# SCOTUSWatch for Android

Get instant push notifications when the U.S. Supreme Court publishes new documents — Order Lists, Slip Opinions, In-Chambers Opinions, and Opinions Relating to Orders.

---

## What It Does

- **Notifies you immediately** when a new Supreme Court document is detected, usually within seconds of it appearing on supremecourt.gov
- **Opens the document** directly in your browser when you tap the notification
- **Works in the background** — no need to keep the app open

## What It Does Not Do

This app is a notification client only. It does **not** include:

- A document browser or archive
- AI summaries or vote breakdowns (those are available on iOS (coming soon), Windows, and Bluesky
- Settings or preferences

---

## Requirements

- Android 8.0 (Oreo) or later
- An internet connection

---

## Installation

SCOTUSWatch is not on the Google Play Store. You install it directly from the APK file — this is called "sideloading" and takes about 30 seconds.

### Step 1 — Download the APK

Go to the **[latest release](https://github.com/scottsapps/SCOTUSWatch_Android/releases/latest)** and tap **SCOTUSWatch.apk** to download it to your phone.

### Step 2 — Allow installation from unknown sources

Android blocks APKs from outside the Play Store by default. When you open the downloaded file, Android will prompt you to allow your browser or file manager to install apps — tap **Settings**, enable it, then go back and tap **Install**.

> The exact wording varies by Android version and manufacturer, but it will always offer to take you to the right setting automatically.

### Step 3 — Install and open

Tap **Install** when prompted, then **Open**. The app will ask for notification permission — tap **Allow**. That's it.

---

## Getting Updates

Updates are not automatic. When a new version is available, download the new APK from the [Releases page](https://github.com/scottsapps/SCOTUSWatch_Android/releases) and install it over the existing app — your settings are preserved.

---

## Privacy

The app registers an anonymous device token with the SCOTUSWatch backend so it can receive push notifications. No personal information is collected or transmitted.
